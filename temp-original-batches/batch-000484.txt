Challah is a type of bread often baked for Jewish celebrations such as Shabbat. Typically, challah is prepared in a way that's pareve in that it doesn't combine dairy products with egg. The bread loaves are braided and, on Rosh Hashanah, are often presented in a round &quot;turban&quot; form. On Shabbat, two loaves of challah are covered with a cloth that can be rather elaborately embroidered or decorated and Kiddush blessings are recited before the bread is sliced and eaten.\n\nDepending upon the occasion, challah may be served sprinkled with salt or with honey and in some cultures raisins, dates, or other ingredients may even be added! No matter how you slice it, this rich and eggy bread is a delicious treat that helps unite people all around the world.\n\nVegetarian-Safe, Lactose Free
